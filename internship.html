Why Intern at Hebron

Real Manufacturing Experience at a Tier-1 Supplier

At Hebron Automotive Products Pvt. Ltd., we believe the best engineers are shaped on the shop floor — not just in classrooms. Our internship programme gives you unmatched exposure to live automotive manufacturing operations, working alongside skilled engineers in a certified, high-pressure production environment.

Founded in 2019, Hebron has grown into a leading Tier-1 supplier with 120+ employees, serving Tier-1 clients like Gabriel India Ltd. and Hitachi Astemo. As an intern, you'll witness and participate in every stage of this value chain.

Live die casting operations — witness high-pressure and gravity die casting of aluminium automotive components in real time, with industry-standard process parameters.
VMC and CNC machining on HAAS USA machines — understand G-code, fixture setups, tool wear monitoring, and how precision tolerances are achieved in production.
IATF 16949 quality systems in practice — learn how SPC, MSA, PPAP, and FMEA are used in daily production, not just as theory, but as live tools to control quality.
Mentorship from senior engineers — every intern is assigned a department mentor who guides project work, answers technical queries, and evaluates performance.
Powder coating and surface treatment processes — understand automotive-grade finishing operations including DFT measurement, adhesion tests, and salt spray testing.
Cross-functional exposure — interact with production, quality, toolroom, maintenance, and supply chain teams to understand how a world-class automotive plant is run end-to-end.

Departments

Choose Your Area of Interest

We offer structured internship placements across six key departments, each providing hands-on technical experience in a live automotive manufacturing environment.

Manufacturing

Production & Die Casting

Learn high-pressure and gravity die casting (PDC/GDC) operations on real production machines. Understand melt preparation, die setup, material handling, injection parameters, cycle time control, and process troubleshooting in a live manufacturing environment.

Machining

CNC / VMC Operations

Operate 5-axis HAAS USA VMC machining centres and CNC turning machines. Gain practical knowledge of G-code programming, fixture and tooling setup, cutting tool selection, offset management, and maintaining tight tolerances on aluminium automotive components.

Quality

Quality & Inspection

Work hands-on with advanced metrology equipment — Video Measuring Machine (VMM), Universal Testing Machine (UTM), Spectrometer, Ra Tester, and 2D measurement. Learn IATF 16949 quality management, SPC charting, MSA studies, and incoming/outgoing inspection protocols.

Design

Design & Engineering

Create and revise CAD drawings for die casting dies, fixtures, and tooling. Participate in process improvement projects, design for manufacturing (DFM) reviews, and engineering change management. Exposure to component drawings, GD&T, and tooling design principles.

Maintenance

Maintenance

Learn preventive and predictive maintenance of die casting machines, hydraulic systems, pneumatic circuits, and electrical control panels. Assist in scheduled maintenance activities, breakdown analysis, MTTR and MTBF tracking, and spare parts management for a 7-machine PDC plant.

Administration

Administration & HR

Understand how a Tier-1 automotive business operates — from vendor management, purchase orders, and supply chain coordination to HR processes including payroll basics, attendance management, statutory compliance, and employee engagement practices in a manufacturing company.

Eligibility

Who Can Apply

We welcome motivated engineering students who are passionate about manufacturing and eager to learn in a fast-paced industrial environment.

Eligibility Criteria

BE / B.Tech students in Mechanical Engineering, Production Engineering, Metallurgy, or Industrial Engineering
Diploma students in Mechanical Engineering, Production Engineering, or Tool & Die Making
Currently enrolled and registered in a recognised university or polytechnic institution
Academic standing of 60% or above in the most recent semester / year examinations
Genuine interest in automotive manufacturing, quality systems, or industrial engineering
Willingness to work on the shop floor and engage with live production processes
Basic knowledge of engineering drawing, tolerances, and manufacturing fundamentals (preferred)
